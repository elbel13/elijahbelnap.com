I am obsessed with Neovim. Like any normal human being, I've shared my enthusiasm with all my friends and family in hopes some of them might agree. Almost all of them don't seem interested. Even more surprising is that most of those who also like Neovim seem just as passionate about it as I am. Why is it that there's such a divide where pretty much everyone seems to either love or be entirely uninterested in Neovim?

The answer is that Neovim simply isn't for everyone. It is an extremely powerful tool and can be life changing if it's a good fit for you, but otherwise it doesn't even make sense to learn. Since I realized this I stopped trying to convince everyone that Neovim is the best thing since sliced bread. Instead, I hope that this article will help you better understand why this tool can be amazing and why or why not you might want to give it a try.

## What Using Neovim Is Like

Neovim is a terminal based text editor and the successor to Vim. What makes it special is its focus on keyboard efficiency. If you have incorporated even the simplest of keyboard shortcuts into your workflow, you probably already have felt some degree of this efficiency. Neovim takes this to the extreme and allows you to do almost anything without ever taking your hands off the keyboard. This can be a huge time saver and will make any other way to edit text feel clumsy in comparison.

For example, when I'm working in the terminal I can use Neovim to open up files as I explore directories without having to switch to a different application. While I'm there I can scroll lines of text or even jump between words without most of my fingers leaving the home row. Sometimes it feels like I can jump to a specific location, make a quick edit, then continue jumping through the file without ever reaching for the mouse.

In comparison with when I have to work in the browser or another program, I feel like using the arrow keys to navigate or switching to the mouse are both painfully slow and inefficient. I can get much more done in a shorter amount of time when I'm using Neovim. It just feels like the most natural way to edit text and I can't imagine going back to anything else.

This feeling only increases when you add in macros, extensions, and other customizations. All of a sudden you feel like you've become a wizard and Neovim is your magic wand. With all this power at your fingertips it almost feels painful when you have to use something else.

## Then What's The Catch?

There is one huge and unavoidable drawback to Neovim: it is unintuitive and has a steep learning curve. It took me several weeks of practice before I could even do the most basic of tasks without referencing a cheat sheet or Googling how to do a particular thing. For some people it may be even more difficult to learn.

There is a built in tutorial that can help you get started, but it is still a lot to take in. You have to learn a whole new set of commands and ways of thinking about editing text. It can be overwhelming at first and may even feel like you're making things more difficult for yourself.

The other drawback is that Neovim lives in the terminal. Many people have built applications and extensions that mimic the functionality of Neovim in other places, but most do not fully implement all of the same functionality. Neovim was built for the terminal and runs best there.

This is actually more of a double edged sword than a drawback. If you are an avid terminal user, Neovim is an amazing tool to use. However, for the rest of the world that shies away from the intimidating TTY interface, running a text editor in the terminal is a dealbreaker.

## When Learning Neovim Actually Makes Sense

Because of the extensive drawbacks, I don't recommend everyone learn Neovim. If you spend a lot of time in the terminal I think the benefits of learning it far outweighs the cost. Even if you aren't in the terminal often it still may be a good fit for you if you're looking for the best ways to optimize the way you use computers.

For me personally, I began learning Neovim because I wanted to spend more time in the terminal with the goal of better optimizing my workflow. It didn't take long on my journey to learn the ins and outs of the terminal to stumble on Neovim and then fall down the rabbit hole of tutorials and tools.

Some even use Neovim as their IDE, but I wouldn't actually recommend that for most people. Even though I have fallen in love with Neovim, I still prefer VSCode as an IDE. One reason for this is that Neovim as an IDE has an even steeper learning curve than Neovim as a simple text editor. Another reason is that there's no standard way to configure it as an IDE, so any implementation tends to be extremely customizable and time consuming to configure.

## The Next Step on Your Neovim Journey

Hopefully at this point you've decided if Neovim sounds like something worth trying out. If you have, I would recommend installing Neovim and beginning the built in tutorial. It's as simple as typing `:Tutor` after launching Neovim.

Do be warned that there isn't necessarily an end destination to learning Neovim. The built in functionality is very rich and the extensibility makes it practically impossible to learn everything there is to know about using it. That's why I would recommend starting with the tutorial and then simply incorporating it into your normal workflow. From there, you can decide what does or doesn't make sense to learn.

There is a part of me that deeply hopes you do try it and end up loving Neovim. It's opened up new possibilities and has changed the way I think about tooling in general. However, another part of me recognizes that many people simply have no need to learn such a daunting text editor.

So if you think Neovim sounds like it might be for you, try the tutorial. If not, that's okay too. Either way I hope you now understand why some people, including myself, are so passionate about it.
